If you do business in Saudi Arabia, you've probably heard the term *local content* more and more. It sits at the heart of Vision 2030, yet it's often treated as a confusing compliance box to tick. It doesn't have to be.

## What it actually measures

At its simplest, local content measures how much of a company's spending stays inside the Saudi economy — local salaries, local suppliers, local manufacturing, training, and investment. The higher your local content percentage, the more value your business is keeping in the Kingdom.

## Why it matters now

Local content is increasingly tied to who wins work. In tenders and government-linked contracts, a stronger local content score can be the difference between being shortlisted and being passed over. What used to be a "nice to have" is becoming a genuine commercial advantage.

## The problem most companies face

The hard part isn't the idea — it's the data. Calculating a local content score means pulling together payroll, procurement, and supplier information that usually lives in different places. Many companies simply don't know their score, let alone how to improve it.
